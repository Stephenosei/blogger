My wife deleted my girlfriend's number and saved her number with it

I find myself in an incredibly complicated and distressing situation, and I am turning to you for advice and guidance. My actions have led to a tangled web of deceit and hurt, and now I am at a crossroads, unsure of how to move forward.

I have been married to my wife for seven years. We have two wonderful children, ages 6 and 3. For the most part, our marriage has been good, but over the past year, I found myself drawn to someone else—a woman I met through work. Let's call her Emma. What started as a professional relationship gradually turned into an emotional affair, and eventually, a physical one. I know what I did was wrong, and I am deeply ashamed.

Emma and I started working closely on a major project about a year ago. We spent long hours together,and I found myself confiding in her about work stresses and even personal issues. She was understanding and supportive, and I felt a connection with her that I hadn’t felt with anyone in a long time. Our conversations became more personal and intimate, and before I knew it, I was spending more time with her outside of work—grabbing coffee, having dinner, and eventually, spending nights together.

This affair gave me a sense of excitement and escape from the routine of my married life. I justified my actions by telling myself that my wife and I had grown distant, that we were more like roommates than partners. But deep down, I knew I was betraying her trust.

A few weeks ago, my wife found out about the affair. She was devastated, and rightly so. We had a massive confrontation, during which she demanded that I end things with Emma immediately. I agreed, recognizing the hurt I had caused and wanting to make amends. I deleted Emma’s number from my phone in front of my wife to show her that I was serious about ending the affair.

However, my wife took things a step further in a way that I had not anticipated. She had found Emma's number in our phone records and saved her own number under Emma's name. I only realized this when I tried to call Emma to officially end things, and my wife answered instead. Her voice on the other end of the line was cold and unforgiving. She had been waiting for me to slip up, and I fell right into her trap.

"I knew you couldn't be trusted," she said, her tone ice-cold. "You were never going to end it, were you?"

In that moment, I realized the extent of my betrayal and the depth of her pain. She had gone through the effort of setting this trap, indicating just how hurt and distrustful she had become. Her actions were not just a test but a clear message: she was done tolerating my deceit.

My wife was furious and heartbroken all over again, and this time, she made it clear that she saw no path to forgiveness. She accused me of not truly wanting to end the affair and of trying to go behind her back. Her trust in me, which was already shattered, has now been completely obliterated.

Now, we are at a critical juncture. My wife is considering divorce, and our home is filled with tension and mistrust. Our children sense the turmoil, and their once happy and carefree demeanor has changed. I deeply regret my actions and want to save my marriage, but I don’t know how to rebuild the trust that I have broken so badly.

Emma, on the other hand, has been trying to reach out to me, confused about why I suddenly stopped all communication. She left me several messages asking for an explanation, but I haven’t had the courage to respond. I know I need to end things with her completely, but I also feel a sense of responsibility towards her because I led her to believe that I had genuine feelings for her.

I am seeking advice from anyone who has been in a similar situation or who can offer guidance on how to navigate this painful and complex path. How can I demonstrate to my wife that I am genuinely committed to ending the affair and rebuilding our relationship? Is it possible to regain her trust, or have I damaged our marriage beyond repair? How can we create a stable and loving environment for our children amidst this chaos? And how do I finally and definitively end things with Emma without causing further harm?

Thank you in advance for your insights and support. Your advice and shared experiences will be invaluable as I try to find a way to heal the wounds I have caused and preserve our family.

-Bra Kofi-
