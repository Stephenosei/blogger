My Best Friend Wants to have a feel of my Partner

My world has been turned upside down by a request and an agreement that have left me feeling hurt, confused, and betrayed. I need help navigating this complex and painful situation.

I have been in a committed relationship with my husband, Alex, for four years. We have a loving and supportive relationship, built on trust and mutual respect. We share a deep connection, and I have always felt secure and happy with Alex. We are planning settling down very soon.

I also have a best friend, Lisa, who has been in my life since childhood. Lisa and I have shared countless memories, secrets, and experiences. She has always been someone I could rely on, and I have always valued her friendship immensely.

Recently, Lisa and I watched a movie that involved a couple allowing a close friend to have a one-time fling with one of the partners. After the movie, Lisa confided in me that she found Alex attractive. She said it jokingly at first, but then she became more serious, telling me that she was curious about what it would be like to be with him. I was taken aback and felt a knot in my stomach. I never expected my best friend to express such feelings about my partner, and I didn't know how to respond.

A few days later, Lisa brought up the topic again, this time suggesting that she wanted to have a "feel" of Alex—just once, to satisfy her curiosity. She insisted that it wouldn't mean anything and that it was purely a physical curiosity. I was horrified by the suggestion and made it clear that I was uncomfortable with the idea.

To my shock, Lisa didn't drop the subject. Instead, she went behind my back and talked to Alex about it. She framed it as a harmless request and, to my utter dismay, Alex agreed. He told me later that he didn't see the harm in it and that it was just a one-time thing. He said he didn't want to hurt Lisa's feelings and thought it might be a way to strengthen our friendship.

Hearing this from Alex felt like a punch to the gut. I felt betrayed by both my best friend and my husband. The people I trusted the most were discussing and agreeing on something that made me feel completely devalued and disrespected. I confronted both of them, expressing my hurt and anger. Lisa brushed it off, saying I was overreacting, and Alex apologized but didn't seem to fully understand the depth of my pain.

Now, I am at a loss. My relationship with Alex feels tainted, and my trust in him is shattered. My friendship with Lisa, which I once cherished, feels irreparably damaged. I am struggling to understand how to move forward from this.

To make matters worse, Alex has been pressuring me to accept Lisa's request. He keeps bringing up the movie we watched and suggesting that it's a modern and open-minded approach to relationships. He says that allowing this one-time thing could actually bring us all closer together and make our bonds stronger. His insistence is making me feel even more confused and hurt.

I am seeking advice from anyone who has been in a similar situation or who can offer guidance on how to navigate this painful and complex dilemma. Is it possible to rebuild trust in my relationship with Alex, or has this incident caused irreversible damage? Should I try to salvage my friendship with Lisa, or is it time to distance myself from someone who could make such a request?

Thank you in advance for your insights and support. Your advice and shared experiences will be invaluable as I try to find a way forward in this heart-wrenching situation.

-Cynthia-
